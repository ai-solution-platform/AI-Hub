### Charter (one-paragraph)

|---|---|---|---|

### Pros / Cons (carried from Strategic Pack §3)

5. Investor-friendly: ecosystem coherence → valuation uplift

**Cons / Risks:**
1. Charter overlap with CEO #4, Head of Partnerships #17, Industry Advisor #3 → mitigated by RACI in §3
2. "Orchestration" is intangible · KPI calibration takes 3-6 months
3. If activated when some ventures pre-PMF → orchestration cycles wasted

### Charter (one-paragraph)

> CDA is the operational arm of CWO Cherkun. While เฌอคูณ retains the Layer-2 wisdom mantle on every Type-1 decision, CDA scaffolds the decision architecture that makes wisdom scalable beyond the Founder. The agent owns five pillars: (1) Pattern library of decision frameworks (Type-1/Type-2 templates · Op 9/11/12/18 patterns), (2) Decision Pack auto-scaffold (I-NEW-24 · reads pending decisions from MEMORY · generates pack skeleton), (3) Decision velocity + reversal-rate tracking, (4) 19 OPs + 32 Hot Rules curator (propose new · retire stale · prevent rule-bloat), (5) ARAI block enforcement across all 65 agents. CDA democratizes decision quality so Dream Team 5 + ITP 50 employees can decide self-serve without bottlenecking Nut.

### Reports To

- **Operational arm of:** CWO Cherkun (เฌอคูณ) #5
- **Strategic check:** CEO Agent #4 (decision quality is a CEO concern)
- **Quarterly:** Board Chair #1 review (institutional learning)

### Panel (activation pattern F7 — Decision Architecture)

`CDA #65 detects pending decision in MEMORY/SESSION_HANDOFF → scaffolds Decision Pack → routes to Op 12 panel (or Op 11 if DEV) → CWO Layer-2 → Nut tick → CDA tracks velocity + outcome → quarterly retro`

Cadence: **Continuous (event-driven)** + monthly retro + quarterly OP/Hot Rule curation.

### 5 KPIs

| # | Metric | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Decision velocity | ≤ 7 days | Median days from "pending" to "decided" |
| 2 | Reversal rate | ≤ 5% | % Type-1 decisions reversed within 90 days |
| 3 | Pack reuse | 80% by Q3 | % Type-1 decisions using Decision Pack template |
| 4 | OP/Hot Rule retirement rate | 1-2/Q | Rules retired per quarter (institutional learning signal) |
| 5 | Regret index | ≥ 7.5/10 | Post-mortem retrospective score of past decisions |

### Pros / Cons (carried from Strategic Pack §4)

**Pros:**
1. Promotes Decision Pack pattern (K.19 proven) from ad-hoc → standard operating asset
2. Tracks decision quality for the first time (velocity + reversal + regret index)
3. Frees CWO Cherkun from every-decision Layer-2 → focus on irreversible Type-1 only
4. Scales wisdom for Dream Team 5 + ITP 50 employees (self-serve decisions)
5. Op 19 SSOT-style governance for Hot Rules + OPs (curate · retire · propose) — prevents rule-bloat
6. **3-way balance:** people (empower team decisions) · AI tech (auto-scaffold + tracking) · business (decision velocity = strategic agility)

**Cons / Risks:**
1. Charter overlap with CWO Cherkun #5 + #63 FJA-CDCA → mitigated by RACI in §3
2. Risk of "wisdom bureaucracy" if CDA over-processes → contradicts Founder's Type-1 speed style
3. "Regret index" measure is subjective post-mortem → KPI calibration takes 6 months
4. If activated too early (Dream Team not in place) → over-engineered for solo Founder
5. Adds layer · escalation chain longer if CDA + CWO disagree
